Launch Camp coaches and guest mentors are experienced entrepreneurs, educators and developers.

Coaches

David Gaynor, CEO School Shaped

David attended Olin College of Engineering where he created and taught the school's first student led course - on web development and entreprenuership. After graduating he worked at Twitter for two and a half years, before leaving to found the nonprofit School Shaped the for-profit Classadoo. In 2015 he was given the opportunity to teach a two week intersession course at Design Tech High School. He decided to teach a condensed Olin College course. The resulting program, Two Week Business would eventually become Launch Camp.

Catherine Uong, Associate at Y Combinator

Catherine has over 10 years of experience mentoring and running volunteer programs for middle and high school students interested in entrepreneurship and project-based learning. She is passionate about creating environments for students to take ownership of their own learning, valuing academic achievement as well as the development of social-emotional skills. Currently, Catherine is an Associate at Imagine K12, an education vertical within the technology startup accelerator, Y Combinator.

Tommy Hoang, Teacher at East Bay Innovation Academy

Tommy has been an educator for the past six years, working with students of all ages and backgrounds. His student-led classroom focuses on whole child development through interdisciplinary projects. Tommy's teaching approach centers around the lean startup process which he learned by developing his own edtech tool last summer. He currently teaches science at East Bay Innovation Academy where he often prototypes solutions to improve communication with families.

Guest Mentors

Rujul Zaparde, CEO of FlightCar

By the time he was a senior in high school Rujul had grown a nonprofit giving 55,000 villagers in India access to clean water. Rujul deferred his acceptance to Harvard after graduation and started FlightCar, a business changing the way people park and rent vehicles at airports. He wants to inspire other teens to “pick a cause and let the light bulb go off”, and we’re excited to have him helping out our teams.

Jialiya Huang, Founder of Tessel

Jialiya joined her first startup freshman year of college and launched her own profitable mobile app two years later. In her senior year of college she worked on a secret project for Facebook, for which she still holds a patent. In 2013 she launched the Tessel, an internet connected microcontroller that is the largest open source project of it’s kind. She is now an engineer at 3D Robotics, a drone company in East Bay. Jialiya will be mentoring Launch Camp students on manufacturing and distribution.

Cameron Ellis, Lead Engineer at Trumaker

Cameron dropped out of college after struggling with the traditional education system. He taught himself programming while holding a steady job and then leveraged his skills by starting various enterprises in his spare time. He’s one heck of a developer and recently started a new business. We're excited for Cameron to start working with our students, especially those with who have digital products.

Steve Roseman, Independent Glass Blower

Steve worked at HP for over 20 years, before leaving to pursue glass blowing and found an electric bike shop. Steve sells his glass at some of the biggest name art shops in San Francisco including Gump's in Union Square. He's excited to help Launch Camp students, especially those who are founding a business around an artistic passion.

Paul Booth, VP of Engineering at Bizzy

Paul Booth has worked at Google, has several years experience leading an Growth Engineering team at Twitter, has published several papers in Discrete Applied Mathematics and now leads Engineering at Bizzy - a recent Y Combinator marketing startup. He's looking forward to visiting Launch Camp to give tips on marketing and growth.
